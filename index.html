Eve of WWI to Creation of the UN

06/08/1914 - The Assassination of Archduke Francis Ferdinand

Archduke Franz Ferdinand and his wife Sophie were shot to death by a Bosnian Serb nationalist during an official visit to the Bosnian capital of Sarajevo. This is important because the killings are believed to have sparked a chain of events that led to the outbreak of World War I by early August.


05/7/1915 - Sinking of the RMS Lusitania

The Lusitania was a British ocean liner travelling from New York to Liverpool in May of 1915. During its journey it was attacked and fatally torpedoed by a German U-boat killing all passengers and crew. This even is significant because it is believed to be a large contributing factor to the United States joining World World I.


06/28/1919 - Treaty of Versailles

This treaty was a peace agreement that helped ultimately bring World War I to an end. This is important because it stated that Germany would be held accountable for actions contained and the war itself. This would cause them to lose money, land, and much more.


4/24/1915-1923 - Armenian Genocide

The Armenian Genocide was a campaign of deportation and mass killing conducted against Armenian subjects of the Ottoman Empire by the Young Turk government. This is important as it occurred during World War I and the Young Turks joined with Germany.


2/19/1915-1/9/1916 - Gallipoli Campaign

This campaign was a plan against the Turks by Great Britain to ultimately knock the Ottoman Empire out of the war. This plan ended up backfiring making it important because Winston Churchill was demoted and the Turks actually won.


10/24/1945 - Creation of United Nations & UDHR

The United Nations was a worldwide international organization to precede the League of Nations formed by the Treaty of Versailles. It is important because it was created to "reaffirm faith in fundamental human rights" establish justice, and more.


Events From Cold War

05/14/1948 - Founding of Israel

On this day, the head of the Jewish Agency proclaim the establishment of the State of Israel & president Harry S. Truman recognized the new nation. This is an important milestone for the Jewish religion and world peace.


05/1/1950 - End of Chinese Civil War

The Chinese Civil War was a war fought between the Republic of China and the forces of the Chinese Communist Party. This event is important because it led to the Communist party gaining control and forcing the Republic to retreat.

The Korean War was a conflict between the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea) and the Republic of Korea (South Korea). This is important because when the Soviet Union got involved it became international and caused the United Nation and United States to also get involved.


8/13/1961 - Construction of the Berlin Wall

The Berlin wall was erected to prevent East Germans from fleeing to West Germany. At the time of construction about 2.5 million had already fled. This is important because it was the United States and Great Britain vs the Soviet Union on each side respectively.


7/20/1969 - Man walks on the Moon!

When Apollo 11 landed on the surface of the moon on July 20th it was a huge world event. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin were the first people to land and walk on the moon. This event was important as it was witness via television by hundreds of millions of people all around the globe. It was a huge turning point in the Cold War.


Decolonization & Independence

1/7/1978-2/11/1979 - Iranian Revolution

The Iranian Revolution was an uprising in Iran that resulted in the toppling of the monarchy and led to the establishment of an Islamic Republic. This was important because it halted the westernization of Iran. It also shows just how fast change can happen.


8/31/1980 - Start of Solidarity Movement

This was a Polish trade union in that starting in the early 1980s. It became the first independent labour union in a country belonging to the Soviets. This event is important because it was a stepping stone for other unions and countries to gain independence.


4/15/1989 - Tiananmen Square Protests

This was where students were holding protests against the Chinese government. This sadly led to some kills being killed. This is important because this day ended up being known as the Tiananmen Square Massacre.
